## 1. Strategic foundation

### 1.1 What we are selling (product truth — never deviate)

- India's first **standardized operational framework for architectural consultancy
  practices**. Not a generic ERP, not a construction PM tool.
- Core promise: *"Run every project the same way — and stop losing fees to revisions
  you never agreed to."*
- **AORMS-Lite** — free forever, up to 3 staff logins, unlimited clients/projects,
  no credit card, native Windows desktop app that runs offline.
- **AORMS-Pro** — GST invoicing, HR & payroll, revision intelligence, AI Studio,
  unlimited seats, cloud-hosted or self-hosted.
- **ESTI** (Embedded Studio Intelligence) — the AI layer that reads the workspace:
  Ask ESTI, the deterministic office-health score (ESTI Pulse), the cognition engine.
- India-native financial logic: GST, TDS, 26AS / AIS / GSTR reconciliation, Indian FY.
- **Earned identity:** AORMS-U (person) and AORMS-C (company) unique IDs are *earned*
  at 100 hours of real usage — never bought, never given at signup.
- Client portals and consultant portals — decisions inside a record, not WhatsApp.

### 1.2 Hard content rules

1. **No fabricated testimonials, no fake numbers.** The landing page itself says:
   *"AORMS is early. Rather than invent testimonials, here is honestly what it
   replaces."* Instagram follows the same rule. Honest before/after framing only.
2. No screenshots containing real client data — use the demo workspace.
3. Price/plan claims must match the live pricing band (Lite free forever / Pro).
4. Tone: a senior architect talking to another architect. Never "SaaS growth-bro."
   Dry wit is fine; hype is not.

### 1.3 Who we are talking to (3 personas)

| Persona | Reality | What stops them sleeping | Hook that lands |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Principal** (35–55, 5–25 staff firm) | Runs the office from memory + WhatsApp + Excel | Unpaid revisions, fee leakage, "only I know everything" | "Never absorb a free revision again" |
| **The Freelancer / young practice** (25–35, 1–3 people) | Freelances *and* collaborates with other firms | Looking unprofessional to clients; chaos at zero budget | "Free forever, offline, no credit card" + one identity across every studio you work with |
| **The Studio Manager / senior architect** | Chases approvals, drawings, timesheets | Nothing is findable; every status question comes to them | "One numbered, dated register for everything issued" |

### 1.4 Positioning against the feed

Indian architecture Instagram is saturated with **render porn**. AORMS should not
compete there. Our lane is the **back office nobody photographs**: the 11pm revision
email, the "small change" that became drawing issue #14, the GST invoice raised
three months late. Pain-recognition content in an aesthetics-driven feed is
pattern-breaking — that is the entire creative strategy.

**Brand voice in one line:** *The practice manual Indian architecture school never taught.*

### 1.5 Visual identity on the grid

- IBM Plex typography, Carbon-token colour discipline (mirror the product).
- Dark, calm, editorial slides — closer to a broadsheet than a startup deck.
- One accent colour per content pillar (see §2) so the grid reads as a system.
- Product UI shown only from the demo workspace; annotate, don't just screenshot.
- Reels: screen-capture + captions + voiceover in Indian-English. No stock footage
  of hard hats — we serve consultancies, not contractors.

## 2. Content pillars

Six pillars. Every post belongs to exactly one. Ratio tuned for a pre-revenue
awareness phase: educate and empathise first, sell second.

| # | Pillar | Share | Job | Formats |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | **The Leak** — fee/revision pain | 25% | Make the invisible loss visible | Reels, single statics |
| P2 | **Practice School** — how to run an office | 25% | Save-worthy education (COA fee stages, GST for architects, transmittals, scope letters) | Carousels |
| P3 | **Product in 30s** — one feature, one problem | 20% | Show, don't claim | Screen-capture reels |
| P4 | **ESTI / AI in practice** | 10% | Differentiate: deterministic score + grounded AI, not chatbot hype | Reels, carousels |
| P5 | **The 100-Hour Identity** | 10% | Brand mythology: IDs are earned, not bought | Statics, stories, milestone posts |
| P6 | **Build-in-public / founder notes** | 10% | Trust without testimonials | Statics, stories, occasional live |

### Pillar details & example angles

**P1 — The Leak** (accent: red)
- "The 'small change' autopsy": trace one verbal *chhota sa change* through 6
  revisions and ₹0 of billing.
- "What a free revision actually costs": hours × your own hourly worth, on screen.
- "Approvals that live in WhatsApp don't exist" — scroll through a fake 400-message
  group hunting for the word 'approved'.

**P2 — Practice School** (accent: blue) — the save/share engine
- COA stage-wise fee structure, explained in 8 slides.
- GST for architectural services: SAC codes, when to raise, what 26AS/AIS/GSTR
  reconciliation actually is (educational, product-agnostic — soft CTA only).
- Anatomy of a drawing transmittal that protects you.
- A scope-of-work letter that survives a dispute.
- Site visit reports that clients actually read.
- TDS every architect gets wrong.
- These rank in search and get saved — the compounding asset.

**P3 — Product in 30s** (accent: green)
- One reel = one pain = one feature. Never a feature tour.
- Examples: raise a GST invoice in 60 seconds · the revision register ·
  client approves inside the portal (timestamped) · the drawing register ·
  works offline on the desktop app · reconcile against 26AS.

**P4 — ESTI** (accent: purple)
- "Your office has a pulse. Can you read it?" — the deterministic pressure score.
- Ask ESTI answering from *your own* project record, not the internet.
- Honest AI framing: "Deterministic systems create the score; ESTI explains it."

**P5 — The 100-Hour Identity** (accent: gold)
- The rule: your AORMS-U ID cannot be bought. 100 hours of real practice earns it.
- Congratulation posts as users cross 100 hours (with permission, handle-only).
- One identity, many studios — built for architects who freelance *and* work
  with other firms.
- This is the community mechanic: an earned credential people will screenshot
  and share themselves.

**P6 — Build-in-public** (accent: neutral)
- Changelog posts ("What we shipped this month").
- Honest "what AORMS replaces" framing: *three WhatsApp groups, a shared
  spreadsheet, and a drawing folder nobody can find* → one record.
- Founder voice notes on why a feature exists (e.g., why revision source is
  tracked as CLIENT_DRIVEN vs INTERNAL_ERROR).

## 3. Posting frequency & cadence

### Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–4): 4 posts/week
Build the grid to ~16 posts so profile visitors see a system, not an experiment.

| Day | Slot (IST) | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | 8:00 pm | P2 carousel (Practice School) |
| Wed | 12:30 pm | P1 or P3 reel |
| Fri | 8:00 pm | P3 reel or P4 |
| Sun | 11:00 am | P5 / P6 static |
| Daily | — | 1–2 stories (polls, this-or-that, behind-the-scenes) |

### Phase 2 — Rhythm (Months 2–3): 5 posts/week
Add a second reel slot (Tue 8 pm). Reels are the reach engine — target **3 reels,
1 carousel, 1 static per week**. Stories daily; one interactive sticker
(poll/quiz/question) at least 3×/week — e.g. *"How many unbilled revisions did
your office absorb last month?"* Poll answers become next month's content.

### Phase 3 — Compounding (Month 4+): 5–6 posts/week
- Add 1 monthly **Instagram Live / collab Live** with a practising architect on an
  office-management topic (not a product demo).
- Convert top-performing Practice School carousels into a pinned "Practice
  School" highlight + guide.
- Repost milestone content: every real AORMS-U-XXXXXX minted at 100 hours.

**Timing rationale (IST):** architects check phones at lunch (12:30–1:30 pm) and
after site/office hours (8–10 pm). Sunday late morning is planning/reading time
for principals. Test and re-tune monthly with Insights.

**Non-negotiables:** never break the pillar ratio for more product posts; reply to
every comment within 24h for the first 6 months; every reel gets captions
(sound-off viewing dominates).

## 4. Concrete post content (ready to produce)

### 4.1 Launch reel — "The ₹3 lakh WhatsApp message" (P1)

**Format:** 30s reel, screen-record style over a mock chat.
**Hook (0–3s):** text on screen — *"This one message cost an architect ₹3,00,000."*
**Body:** a client bubble: *"Ek chhota sa change karna tha…"* → cut to a folder of
files: `FINAL_rev6_ACTUAL-FINAL_v2.dwg` → voiceover: "No revision record. No
approval trail. No extra fee. Sound familiar?"
**Close:** "AORMS keeps a numbered, dated register of every revision — and whether
you agreed to absorb it." → *Link in bio. Free for small practices.*
**Caption:**
> Architects don't lose money because they can't design. They lose it because the
> office runs on memory. Every revision in AORMS carries a category and a source —
> client-driven or internal — so 'one small change' is never invisible again.
> AORMS-Lite is free forever for up to 3 staff. No credit card. Link in bio.

### 4.2 Carousel — "The COA fee stages, actually explained" (P2, 8 slides)

1. **Cover:** "You quoted a lump sum. COA gave you stages. Here's the map."
2. Concept design — what % and what you must deliver.
3. Preliminary design & drawings.
4. Statutory approvals — the stage everyone under-bills.
5. Working drawings & tender.
6. Construction supervision — periodic vs continuous.
7. Completion. Slide text: "Each stage = an invoiceable event. Are you invoicing
   by stage or by mood?"
8. **CTA:** "AORMS tracks fee stages per project and tells you what's earned but
   uninvoiced. Free Lite edition — link in bio."

### 4.3 Reel — "Raise a GST invoice in 60 seconds" (P3)

Screen-capture from demo workspace: pick project → fee stage → SAC code and GST
auto-applied → PDF out. Voiceover: "From 'we should bill them' to a GST-compliant
PDF, before your chai gets cold." Caption notes Lite = simple non-GST invoicing,
GST invoicing on Pro — say it straight, the honesty *is* the brand.

### 4.4 Static — "The 100-hour rule" (P5)

Black slide, gold monospace text:
> **AORMS-U-______**
> You can't buy this ID.
> You can't get it at signup.
> 100 hours of running your practice on AORMS earns it.
> Some things should still be earned.

Caption explains earned identity + one identity across every studio you work
with (freelancers rejoice). This is the most screenshot-able brand asset we have.

### 4.5 Carousel — "What AORMS replaces" (P6, honest before/after)

- Slide 1: "We won't show you fake testimonials." (verbatim brand stance)
- Slide 2–3 (BEFORE): three WhatsApp groups · a shared spreadsheet ·
  a drawing folder nobody can find · approvals in someone's memory.
- Slide 4–5 (AFTER): one project record · a numbered drawing register ·
  client approvals timestamped in a portal · fees tied to stages.
- Slide 6: "AORMS is early. That's exactly why the free Lite edition exists —
  grow with us." → CTA.

### 4.6 Reel — "Your office has a pulse" (P4)

Open on the ESTI Pulse score animating. VO: "This number isn't an AI guess. It's
computed from your live office record — deadlines, load, approvals, revisions.
Deterministic systems create the score. ESTI explains it. Ask it why your
Tuesday feels like it does."

### 4.7 Reel — "When everything is urgent" (P4, ESTI · square)

Animated 37s reel, produced — source in [`reels/`](reels/)
(`task-prioritization-square.html`, 1080×1080). Hook: five red "URGENT" site
chips pile up → *"Everything's urgent. So what comes first?"* Body: the ESTI
daily stand-up gathers site updates in chat (*"No site supervisor? Any team
member can report."*) → ESTI weighs client priority, site dependencies and
deadline proximity → a ranked task list with the reasoning under each row
(*"Do this, in this order."*). Payoff: *"Stop guessing what's next."* End
card: *"Every site, scored. Every morning, a plan. ESTI · inside AORMS Pro."*

### 4.8 Story sequences (recurring)

- **Poll:** "Last month, how many revisions did you do for free?" (0 / 1–3 / 4–10 /
  I stopped counting) → follow-up story: "The 'stopped counting' gang — this
  register is for you" + product frame.
- **Quiz:** "Which SAC code applies to architectural services?" → answer + save-able
  card.
- **This-or-that:** WhatsApp approval vs portal approval, dated & signed.
- **Friday feature:** 15-second screen capture of one small thing shipped that week.

## 6. Growth mechanics beyond the grid

1. **Engage before you post (the 30/30 rule):** 30 minutes/day commenting
   substantively from the brand account on Indian architecture pages, practice-
   management conversations, and COA/IIA-adjacent posts. Comments from a page
   named "AORMS · Architecture Office OS" are themselves ads.
2. **Collab posts:** monthly collab (co-authored post/Live) with a practising
   architect or architecture educator on office management — their audience
   becomes ours via the collab mechanic. No payment, no scripted praise; the
   topic is the value.
3. **The 100-hour flywheel:** when a user earns AORMS-U, offer (never require) a
   congratulation collab post. Earned IDs are shareable achievements — this is
   organic UGC that cannot be faked, perfectly aligned with the no-testimonial rule.
4. **DM automation kept human:** auto-reply keyword "LITE" on launch posts → DM
   with the aorms.in signup link. Everything else answered by a human.
5. **Cross-surface:** every Practice School carousel becomes a blog post on
   aorms.in (SEO) and a LinkedIn document post (principals live there too).
6. **Bio:** `India's OS for architecture practices · Projects, fees, GST, revisions
   — one record · Lite: free forever ↓` + link to aorms.in (later a link-in-bio
   page: signup / download / Practice School index).

## 7. Measurement

| Phase | North-star | Guardrails |
|---|---|---|
| Weeks 1–4 | Profile visits → link clicks | ≥3 posts/wk shipped; reply rate 100% |
| Months 2–3 | Saves on P2 carousels (save rate ≥5%) | Reel reach trend; follower quality (India %, role) |
| Month 4+ | **Lite signups attributed to Instagram** (UTM `?utm_source=ig`) | 100-hour IDs earned by IG-attributed cohort |

Monthly review ritual: top 3 / bottom 3 posts by save-rate and by link clicks →
double down / kill format. Hashtag set pruning (§5). Story poll answers → next
month's P1/P2 topics.

**What we do not chase:** follower count, viral reach outside India, engagement-
bait. One principal who signs up is worth 10,000 render-scrollers.
