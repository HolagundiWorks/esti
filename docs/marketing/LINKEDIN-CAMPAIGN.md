**Product:** AORMS (**Accelerated Operational Resources Management System**, platform; **AORMS-Studio** workspace) — aorms.in
**By:** Holagundi Consulting Works
**Audience geography:** India (primary: Bengaluru + metro practice hubs)

**Companion doc:** [`INSTAGRAM-CAMPAIGN.md`](INSTAGRAM-CAMPAIGN.md) — shared product

---

## 1. Why LinkedIn is a different campaign, not a repost channel

Instagram reaches architects scrolling for aesthetics; LinkedIn reaches the
people who **sign the cheque**: principals, partners, and studio managers in
work mode. Three structural differences drive everything below:

1. **The founder profile outperforms the company page 5–10× on reach.**
   LinkedIn's algorithm favours people over pages. The primary publishing
   surface is the **founder's personal profile**; the AORMS company page is the
   credibility anchor (follow target, jobs, product tab), not the reach engine.
2. **Text is a first-class format.** A well-written 1,200-character story about
   an unbilled revision outperforms a designed graphic. Document posts (PDF
   carousels) are the save/share engine. Native video is rising but optional.
3. **Dwell time is the ranking currency.** Long, structured posts that keep a
   reader on-post beat link-first posts. **Links go in the first comment or at
   the very end** — never in the opening lines.

**Positioning line for LinkedIn:** *The business of architecture, finally treated
like a business.* Same voice as Instagram — a senior architect talking to
another architect — but here we lean harder into money, contracts, and
operations, because that is what this audience opens LinkedIn for.

### Hard content rules (inherited, non-negotiable)

1. **No fabricated testimonials, no fake numbers, no invented case studies.**
   Honest before/after framing only — "what AORMS replaces", never "what client
   X achieved" unless a real, named, consenting user says it themselves.
2. Product screenshots only from the demo workspace.
3. Plan claims must match the live pricing band: **AORMS-Lite** free forever
   (3 staff logins, unlimited projects/clients, offline Windows desktop app);
   **AORMS-Pro** adds GST invoicing, HR/payroll, revision intelligence,
   AI Studio, unlimited seats, self-hosting.
4. AI claims stay honest: *deterministic systems create the score; ESTI
   explains it.* No "AI will run your firm" hype.

## 2. Audience & account architecture

### Personas (LinkedIn-specific weighting)

| Persona | Why they're on LinkedIn | Content that converts them |

| **Principal / Partner** (5–50 staff) — *primary* | Peer visibility, hiring, business development | Fee leakage, revision economics, "stop being the only one who knows everything" |

| Surface | Role | Cadence |
|---|---|---|

## 3. Content pillars (LinkedIn mix)

Same six-pillar system as Instagram, re-weighted for a business audience and
mapped to LinkedIn-native formats:

| # | Pillar | Share | Primary format |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | **The Leak** — fee/revision economics | 25% | Text story posts |
| P2 | **Practice School** — running an office | 30% | Document posts (PDF carousels), newsletter |
| P3 | **Product in context** | 15% | Short native video / annotated screenshot posts |
| P4 | **ESTI / honest AI** | 10% | Text + screenshot posts |
| P5 | **The 100-Hour Identity** | 5% | Milestone posts, celebration reposts |
| P6 | **Build-in-public / founder notes** | 15% | Text posts from the founder profile |

Notes vs Instagram: P2 grows (LinkedIn rewards utility), P6 grows (founder
story is the reach engine), P5 shrinks (mythology plays better on IG; here it
appears as real milestone moments). P3 shrinks and changes shape — LinkedIn
punishes demo-reel content that smells like an ad; product appears *inside a
story about a problem*, one feature at a time.

### Pillar angles tuned for LinkedIn

**P1 — The Leak** (the conversation starter)
- "The revision ledger no Indian practice keeps": walk one verbal *chhota sa
  change* through six drawing issues and ₹0 of billing — as a text narrative,
  not a graphic.
- "Your fee didn't shrink. It leaked." — scope creep vs scope change,
  CLIENT_DRIVEN vs INTERNAL_ERROR, and why the distinction is money.
- "WhatsApp is where approvals go to die" — the discoverability problem framed
  as professional risk, not just annoyance.

**P2 — Practice School** (the authority builder — this is what gets AORMS
followed by people who haven't bought anything yet)
- COA stage-wise fees: a document post mapping stages → invoiceable events.
- GST for architectural services: SAC codes, when liability arises, what
  26AS / AIS / GSTR reconciliation catches. Educational first, product last.
- TDS every architect gets wrong.
- Anatomy of a transmittal that protects you in a dispute.
- A scope-of-work letter that survives arbitration.
- The five-level access ladder: who in your office should see the numbers?
- Each becomes: LinkedIn document post → newsletter section → aorms.in blog
  post (SEO) → Instagram carousel. Write once, publish four times.

**P3 — Product in context**
- Never "look at our features". Always "here is the problem → here is the
  register/record that kills it", with one annotated demo-workspace screenshot
  or a ≤60s screen capture.
- Examples: the revision register with category + source · client approval
  timestamped in the portal · fee stages showing earned-but-uninvoiced ·
  26AS reconciliation matching · the offline desktop app (data on your machine).

**P4 — ESTI / honest AI**
- "Most 'AI for AEC' is a chatbot wearing a hard hat." Contrast with ESTI:
  deterministic office-pulse score computed from the live record; the AI only
  explains it and answers questions grounded in *your* projects.
- This contrarian-but-honest framing is built for LinkedIn's AI-fatigued feed.

**P5 — The 100-Hour Identity**
- Founder post explaining the design decision: why AORMS-U / AORMS-C IDs are
  earned at 100 hours of real usage, never sold, never given at signup.
- Real milestone reposts (with permission): a user's earned ID is a credential
  they'll post themselves — congratulate and repost. Unfakeable social proof,
  fully aligned with the no-testimonial rule.

**P6 — Build-in-public / founder notes** (highest-reach pillar on LinkedIn)
- Why I'm building an OS for Indian architecture practices — the origin post.
- Design-decision posts: why revisions carry a source field; why Lite is free
  forever; why the desktop app works offline; why we won't invent testimonials.
- Monthly changelog: "What shipped in AORMS this month" (company page +
  founder repost).
- Honest struggle posts perform disproportionately well — "what we got wrong
  about X" builds more trust than any feature announcement.

## 4. Posting frequency & cadence

### Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–4)
**Founder: 3 posts/week · Company page: 1–2/week**

| Day | Slot (IST) | Surface | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tue | 9:00 am | Founder | P1 or P6 text post |
| Thu | 9:00 am | Founder | P2 document post |
| Sat | 11:00 am | Founder | P4 / P5 / P6 lighter post |
| Wed | 12:30 pm | Company | P3 product-in-context or changelog |
| Fri | 9:00 am | Company | Repost founder's Thursday document with added context |

Timing rationale: LinkedIn India engagement peaks Tue–Thu 8–11 am (pre-work and
commute reading) with a secondary lunch window 12–1:30 pm. Weekends are low-reach
but low-competition — good for reflective P6 posts. Test and re-tune monthly.

### Phase 2 — Rhythm (Months 2–3)
- Founder to **4 posts/week** (add Mon 9 am P2/P1 slot).
- Launch the monthly **"Practice Notes" newsletter** (first issue = best-performing
  Practice School content, expanded).
- Start **1 comment-engagement hour daily** (see §6) — on LinkedIn this is not
  optional; comments from the founder on others' posts are the top-of-funnel.

### Phase 3 — Compounding (Month 4+)
- Add **1 LinkedIn Live or Audio event/month**: practice-management conversation
  with a practising principal (same guest sourcing as the Instagram collab Lives).
- Convert the top newsletter issues into gated-nothing PDF guides featured on
  the company page (no email-wall — the free Lite signup is the only ask).
- Milestone cadence: repost every earned AORMS-U/AORMS-C identity (with consent).

**Non-negotiables:**
- Links in first comment or post-end, never the opening line.
- Reply to every comment within 24h; each reply is itself a mini-post.
- Never post identical copy on the same day across founder + company page
  (the algorithm suppresses duplicates); the page reposts with added context
  next day.
- Document posts: 8–12 pages, first page must work as a standalone hook.

## 5. Concrete post content (ready to publish)

### 5.1 Founder origin post (P6 — campaign opener)

> Architects don't fail because they can't design.
>
> I watched practices lose lakhs every year — not to bad clients, but to their
> own offices. A "small change" absorbed for free. An approval that lived in a
> WhatsApp group with 400 messages. A GST invoice raised three months after the
> work, because nobody was sure which fee stage was actually complete.
>
> The office ran on memory. And memory doesn't scale, doesn't invoice, and
> doesn't hold up in a dispute.
>
> So we built AORMS — an operating system for Indian architecture practices.
> One record for projects, revisions, approvals, fees, and GST. Not a generic
> ERP with "construction" bolted on. Built only for how Indian consultancy
> practices actually work: COA fee stages, SAC codes, 26AS reconciliation,
> client approvals that carry a timestamp instead of a scroll position.
>
> The Lite edition is free forever — 3 staff logins, unlimited projects, runs
> offline on your own machine. Because a two-person studio in its first year
> needs a real record more than anyone, and can afford it least.
>
> We're early. We won't show you fake testimonials or invented case studies.
> What I will show you, every week here, is exactly what we're building and why.
>
> If your office runs on memory, I'd genuinely like to hear what leaks the most:
> revisions, approvals, or invoicing?
>
> (Link to AORMS in the comments.)

### 5.2 Text post — "The revision ledger" (P1)

> Count your last project's revisions. Now count how many you invoiced.
>
> For most Indian practices the honest answers are "I stopped counting" and
> "zero". Every revision has a source — the client changed their mind, a
> consultant raised a technical query, or the office made an error. Only one of
> those three should ever be free.
>
> But if revisions live in email threads and drawing filenames
> (FINAL_rev6_ACTUAL-FINAL_v2.dwg — you know the one), you can't tell which was
> which. So the practice absorbs all of them, and calls it "client servicing".
>
> A revision register needs four columns: what changed, who drove it, what
> category it falls in, and whether you agreed to absorb it. That's it. Keep it
> from day one of a project and fee conversations stop being awkward — they
> become arithmetic.
>
> This is exactly why every revision in AORMS carries a category (minor / major
> / critical) and a source (client-driven / internal error / technical query /
> scope change). Not for bureaucracy. So the leak becomes visible while it can
> still be billed.
>
> Free Lite edition for small practices — link in the first comment.

### 5.3 Document post — "COA fee stages → invoiceable events" (P2, 10 pages)

Page 1 (hook): **"You quoted a lump sum. COA gave you stages. This map is the
difference between cash flow and hope."** Pages 2–8: each stage, its deliverable,
and its invoice trigger. Page 9: "Earned but uninvoiced — the number most
practices can't produce." Page 10: soft CTA — "AORMS tracks fee stages per
project and shows earned-vs-invoiced per client. Lite is free forever. Link in
comments."

### 5.4 Text post — the honest-AI contrarian (P4)

> Most "AI for architecture" is a chatbot wearing a hard hat.
>
> Ask it about your office and it will answer confidently — from the internet,
> not from your projects. We took the opposite bet with ESTI, the intelligence
> layer inside AORMS:
>
> The office-pressure score is deterministic. Deadlines, team load, pending
> approvals, revision churn — computed from your live record, the same way
> every time. No model "vibes". Then the AI does the one thing it's actually
> good at: explaining the number and answering questions grounded in your own
> project data.
>
> Deterministic systems create the score. ESTI explains it.
>
> If an AI tool can't tell you *why* it said what it said, it doesn't belong
> anywhere near your fee decisions.

### 5.5 Founder post — "Why you can't buy our ID" (P5)

> We made a strange product decision: you cannot buy an AORMS identity.
>
> Every user eventually gets a permanent handle — AORMS-U-XXXXXX — that follows
> them across every studio they work with. Architects freelance, collaborate,
> move between firms; the identity is theirs, not their employer's.
>
> But it isn't granted at signup. It's minted at 100 hours of real usage.
> Not payment. Not a form. A hundred hours of actually running your practice
> on the record.
>
> Why? Because a professional identity should mean something. Anyone can create
> an account; the ID says "this person genuinely works this way". Firms earn
> their AORMS-C the same way — 100 hours of company usage.
>
> Some things should still be earned. Congratulations to everyone who's crossed
> the line so far — you'll know who you are by the six characters after the dash.

### 5.6 Company-page changelog template (P6, monthly)

> **Shipped in AORMS — [Month]**

> • [feature] — [one line]
> • [fix/improvement] — [one line]
> Every item exists because a practice hit the problem. Full notes: aorms.in

### 5.7 Poll posts (1–2/month, founder profile)

## 6. Growth mechanics beyond posting

1. **The daily comment hour (founder, 30–60 min).** Substantive comments on
   posts by Indian architects, practice owners, AEC-tech voices, and COA/IIA-
   adjacent conversations. On LinkedIn, thoughtful comments routinely out-reach
   the original post and are the single fastest way to put "Building AORMS…"
   (the headline) in front of principals. This is the actual top-of-funnel.
2. **Connection strategy, not connection spam.** 10–15 connection requests/day
   to architects who engaged with AORMS content or comments — always with a
   note, never with a pitch. The feed does the selling.
3. **Employee & collaborator amplification.** Everyone associated with
   Holagundi reposts key posts within 90 minutes with one added personal
   sentence (early engagement velocity drives distribution).
4. **Newsletter as owned reach.** "Practice Notes" — monthly, practice-
   management-first, product-last. LinkedIn notifies subscribers directly,
   bypassing feed decay. Repurposes Practice School content.
5. **Milestone flywheel.** Every earned AORMS-U/AORMS-C (with consent):
   congratulate → repost → tag. Unfakeable proof of real usage, compounding.
6. **DM discipline.** No cold pitching, ever. Inbound "how does Lite work?"
   DMs answered by a human within hours; the only link ever sent is aorms.in
   with UTM tags.
7. **Cross-surface loop.** LinkedIn document post ↔ Instagram carousel ↔
   aorms.in blog ↔ newsletter — one Practice School asset, four surfaces,
   produced in one batch.

## 7. Hashtag strategy (LinkedIn rules, not Instagram rules)

LinkedIn hashtags are a **weak** ranking signal — 3–5 per post maximum; more
reads as spam and depresses reach. They matter most for page-follow topics and
search. Every post: 1–2 branded + 2–3 topical.

**Branded (every post):** `#AORMS` · `#PracticeNotes` (newsletter posts) ·
`#100HourID` (milestone posts)

**Topical rotation:**

| Content | Tags |
|---|---|
| P1 / P2 practice & money | `#ArchitecturePractice` `#BusinessOfArchitecture` `#ArchitectsOfIndia` `#PracticeManagement` |
| GST / fees / compliance | `#GST` `#TDS` `#ArchitectFees` `#MSME` |
| P3 / P4 product & AI | `#AECTech` `#ArchTech` `#AIinAEC` `#SaaS` |
| P6 founder / startup | `#BuildInPublic` `#IndianStartups` `#MakeInIndia` `#Bootstrapped` |
| City/community | `#BangaloreArchitects` `#BengaluruStartups` (rotate city with content) |

Avoid mega-generic tags (`#architecture`, `#design`, `#AI` alone) — same logic
as Instagram: global reach, zero Indian principals.

## 8. LinkedIn ads (optional accelerant — only after organic is proven)

Do **not** start ads before Month 3; organic content is the message-market-fit
lab. When ready, smallest viable program:

1. **Retarget only, first.** Sponsored-content retargeting of company-page
   visitors and video viewers with the best organic P2 document + Lite CTA.
   Budget: ₹15–25k/month test.
2. **Thought-leader ads** (boosting the founder's best organic posts) beat
   brand ads on CPM and credibility — use the origin post (§5.1) and the
   revision-ledger post (§5.2).
3. Audience: India · job titles Principal Architect / Partner / Director /
  Studio Manager · industry Architecture & Planning · company size 2–200.
4. Objective: traffic to aorms.in with UTM (`utm_source=li&utm_medium=paid`).
   Judge on Lite signups, never on impressions.

## 9. Measurement

| Phase | North-star | Guardrails |
|---|---|---|
| Weeks 1–4 | Founder-post impressions + profile views trend | 3 posts/wk shipped; 100% comment replies |
| Months 2–3 | Document-post saves & newsletter subscribers | Follower quality (India %, role mix); comment depth |
| Month 4+ | **Lite signups attributed to LinkedIn** (`utm_source=li`) | 100-hour IDs earned by LI-attributed cohort; Live attendance |

Monthly ritual (same as Instagram): top/bottom 3 posts by dwell-proxy
(comments + saves) and by link clicks → double down / kill; poll answers →
next month's topics; hashtag pruning.

**What we do not chase:** connection count, viral reach outside India,
engagement-bait ("Agree?"), AI-generated comment farms. One principal who
signs up outweighs 10,000 impressions.
