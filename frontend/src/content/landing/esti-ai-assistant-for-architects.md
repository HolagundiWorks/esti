The ESTI AI assistant for architects is not a generic chatbot bolted onto a
website. It is designed to read the operating record inside AORMS: projects,
client revisions, approvals, fees, billing, contractor workflows, team workload
and dashboard signals.

That context matters. An architecture office does not need AI that sounds clever
but knows nothing about the current work. It needs assistance that can explain
which project is blocked, which client decision is overdue, which fee needs
follow-up and which team member is overloaded.

## What ESTI AI helps with

ESTI AI can support:

- Dashboard interpretation
- Next-action recommendations
- Client revision context
- Fee and billing follow-up
- Project risk summaries
- Team workload reasoning
- Drafting office notes from existing evidence

The assistant works best when the office record is clean. That is why AORMS
focuses first on structured project, client, fee and approval data.

## AI with evidence

For professional practice, AI should not invent office truth. It should point back
to the record. A useful recommendation should explain what it observed: a delayed
approval, a repeated revision, an unbilled stage, an overloaded owner or an
unresolved contractor item.

This evidence-first approach keeps the principal in control. The assistant can
recommend, explain and draft, but humans still approve decisions.

## Connected to the cognitive dashboard

The cognitive dashboard turns office state into signals. ESTI AI helps interpret
those signals. Instead of showing only numbers, AORMS can explain why attention is
needed and what the next action might be.

## Why context matters more than cleverness

An assistant that cannot read the office record is only guessing. Architecture
practice depends on context: which drawing is current, which client approved what,
which fee stage is complete, which team member owns the next action, and which
contractor item is waiting for review. Without that context, an AI answer may
sound confident while being operationally useless.

ESTI AI is designed to sit on top of structured records. It does not replace the
need for careful project data; it rewards it. The better the project, fee, client
and team records are, the more useful the assistant becomes.

## What a principal can ask

A principal architect might ask:

- What needs attention today?
- Which approvals are holding work?
- Which completed stages should become invoices?
- Which team member is overloaded?
- Which client revision may affect fee or time?
- What should be reviewed before the next project meeting?

These are management questions, not novelty prompts. The value of ESTI AI is that
it can help the principal read the office without opening ten separate modules.

## Human judgement remains central

AORMS is designed for professional offices, so the assistant does not become an
uncontrolled actor. It can explain, recommend and draft. The practice still
decides. That distinction matters in architecture, where communication, liability
and client trust require human judgement.

Used well, ESTI AI is a quieter layer: it helps the office notice what is easy to
miss, and it gives the team a better starting point for the next action.

## A careful assistant for a professional office

Architecture practices do not need an assistant that turns every page into a
performance. They need one that is careful with context, restrained in language
and useful in review. ESTI AI is positioned as office intelligence, not theatre.
It should help a partner prepare for a meeting, help a project lead understand
blockers, or help finance see which completed work needs attention.

The assistant is most valuable when it reduces the principal's need to chase the
same facts again and again. It can gather the current state, explain the risk and
suggest the next responsible action. The office still decides what to do.

## Where it should not replace people

ESTI AI should not replace client judgement, design authorship, professional
responsibility or commercial approval. Those remain human duties. Its proper role
is to read the record and make the next review easier: what moved, what is stuck,
what is exposed and what deserves attention today.

## Why architects may trust it slowly

Trust in office AI should be earned slowly. AORMS makes that possible by grounding
assistant output in visible records rather than mysterious claims. A principal can
compare the recommendation with the project, approval, fee or team record that
caused it.

That makes ESTI AI useful as a disciplined reader of the office. It is not there
to make the practice sound modern. It is there to help the practice notice the
work that needs care.
