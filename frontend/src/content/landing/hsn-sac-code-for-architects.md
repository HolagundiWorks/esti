## HSN / SAC Code for Architecture Services

If you have searched for an **HSN code for architecture**, the short answer is that you probably need a **SAC code** instead. HSN (Harmonised System of Nomenclature) codes classify *goods*; SAC (Services Accounting Code) codes classify *services*. Architectural and design work is a service, so a GST invoice for architecture carries a SAC, not an HSN.

> This is general guidance, not tax advice. Codes and notes change � confirm the exact current SAC and any required digit length with your tax adviser.

## The architectural-services SAC

Architectural services sit in the **9983** group ("Other professional, technical and business services"). Within it, the architecture-specific codes commonly used are in the **998321�998329** range, for example:

- **998321** � architectural advisory services
- **998322** � architectural services for residential building projects
- **998323** � architectural services for non-residential building projects
- **998324 / 998325 / 998326** � historical-restoration, urban-planning and related work

Most design practices invoice under the 9983 / 99832x codes. The correct sub-code depends on the exact nature of the work, so confirm yours with your adviser rather than copying one blindly.

## Why it is a SAC and not an HSN

The confusion is understandable � accounting software, e-commerce and most invoice templates foreground "HSN", because they were built for goods. But for a pure architecture practice there is no product being supplied; you are supplying professional services. Putting a goods HSN on a services invoice is simply the wrong classification, and it is the kind of detail a generic billing tool will happily let you get wrong.

A handful of practices that also *supply goods* (say, fabricated items or materials as part of a works contract) may genuinely need an HSN alongside the SAC. That is a works-contract question for your adviser; for design and consultancy fees, it is a SAC.

## Where the code goes on the invoice

The SAC belongs on every taxable invoice, alongside the 18% GST split (CGST/SGST for intra-state, IGST for inter-state) and the client's TDS deduction under section 194J. Getting all three right on each invoice is what keeps your books matching Form 26AS through the year. The full picture is on the [GST on architecture work](/gst-on-architecture-services) page.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is there an HSN code for architecture services?

No � architecture is a service, so it uses a SAC (Services Accounting Code) from the 9983 family, not an HSN code, which is for goods. AORMS puts the SAC on architectural invoices by default.

### What is the SAC code for architectural services?

Architectural services fall under SAC 9983, commonly 998321�998329 (for example 998322 for residential and 998323 for non-residential building projects). The exact sub-code depends on the work � confirm yours with your tax adviser.

### When might an architect need an HSN code?

Only if the practice also supplies goods � for example as part of a works contract � might an HSN be required alongside the SAC. For pure design and consultancy fees, a SAC is correct.

### Does the SAC code affect the GST rate?

Architectural services are taxed at 18% GST regardless of the specific 99832x sub-code. The code identifies the service for classification and returns; the rate is the same. See [GST on architecture work](/gst-on-architecture-services).
