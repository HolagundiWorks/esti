A principal running a busy practice rarely struggles with any single project. The trouble is the firm. Eleven jobs are live, three are waiting on a client decision, two invoices are overdue, one senior is drowning while a junior is idle, and the only way to know the true position is to ask everyone and stitch the answers together by hand. **Architecture firm management software** exists to remove that stitching — to give the person responsible for the whole office one honest view instead of a dozen partial ones.

AORMS is built for that view. It is not a drawing tool and not an accounting package; it is the system that connects the work, the money and the people so the principal can see the firm without a status meeting.

## The problem with running a firm on fragments

Most firms grow into a stack of disconnected tools. Drawings sit in folders. Fees live in a spreadsheet. Billing happens in a separate accounting program. Approvals run through chat. Client conversations are in email. Each tool is fine on its own, but no tool knows about the others, so the firm-level questions have no answer:

- Which projects are actually at risk this month?
- How much fee is invoiced but not yet collected?
- Who is over capacity and who can take on the next job?
- Are revisions quietly eating the margin on a fixed-fee project?

These are the questions a business owner asks, and a folder of files cannot answer any of them.

## What you can run across the firm

### Every project, at a glance

Each project carries its phases, a drawing register with revisions and approval state, transmittals, and measurement sheets. Across the office, the dashboard rolls those up into computed KPIs, an Action Center of things needing attention, and health modules that surface trouble before a client does. You stop discovering problems in the review meeting.

### The fee pipeline and cash flow

Fees in AORMS follow the COA Scale of Charges, and billing is tied to phase milestones — so a fee proposal becomes a sequence of invoices that fire as the work is actually delivered, not whenever someone remembers. Invoices are GST-correct out of the box: CGST/SGST or IGST split, TDS under section 194J, the professional-services SAC code, and gap-free FY-sequential numbering across the April–March year. You can reconcile collections against bank, 26AS, AIS and GSTR imports, raise purchase orders, and pull GSTR-1, GSTR-3B and TDS filing abstracts when filing season comes. Money is held in paise, so the totals tie out to the rupee.

For the owner this means one number that matters: invoiced versus collected, project by project. See more on [fee proposals and COA-compliant billing](/coa-compliant-billing-software).

### Team utilisation and workload

A firm's capacity is its people. AORMS keeps a team roster, project assignments, a workload overview, attendance, leave and payroll in the same system as the work. You can see who is committed to what, who has slack, and who is quietly carrying three jobs. Tasks carry a classification (billable, non-billable, training, collaboration, personal) and a work type (design communication, design development, technical production, construction support), so utilisation is real rather than guessed.

Performance is scored with ASPRF — a fair, multi-dimensional 30-day composite across Reliability, Quality, Client Impact, Collaboration, Learning and Wellbeing. It is built to reward the right behaviour rather than raw hours, and it gives the principal an honest read on the team without a subjective annual review.

### Revision risk, rolled up

Revisions are where fixed-fee firms bleed. In AORMS every revision is tagged with a category — MINOR, MAJOR or CRITICAL — and a source — client-driven, internal error, technical query or scope change. That intelligence rolls up to the office dashboard, so the principal can see which projects are absorbing uncompensated change and which clients keep moving the brief. It turns a vague feeling that "this job is hard" into a number you can act on, and bill against. There is more on this in [architecture revision tracking](/architecture-revision-tracking).

## Statutory context stays on the record

Risk on the statutory side is firm risk too. AORMS keeps permit notes, authority context, project evidence and client commitments attached to the job, so regulatory assumptions are not left in a consultant's head or a forgotten spreadsheet.

## Sharing without losing control

A firm deals with outsiders constantly. AORMS gives you separate read-only portals for clients, consultants and contractors — they see only what you choose to share, and clients submit their decisions and approvals through the portal so those choices land back in the project record. Nothing is lost in email, and the audit log and activity timeline keep an immutable record of who decided what and when.

## Frequently asked questions

### What does architecture firm management software actually do?

Architecture firm management software connects the parts of the firm that usually live in separate tools — projects and drawings, fees and invoicing, and team workload — into one system. The point is to give the principal a single, honest view across every live job, the cash position and the people, without chasing each team for a status update.

### How is this different from accounting software?

Accounting software records money after the fact. AORMS ties billing to the work itself: fees follow the COA Scale of Charges, invoices fire on phase milestones, and you can reconcile collections against bank, 26AS, AIS and GSTR data. It produces GST-correct invoices and filing abstracts, but it does so from the project record rather than as a detached ledger.

### Can I see which projects are losing money on revisions?

Yes. Every revision is tagged by category and source, and that revision risk rolls up to the office dashboard. A principal can see at a glance which projects are absorbing client-driven or scope-change revisions, which makes it possible to renegotiate fees or have the right conversation before the margin is gone.

### How does it help with team utilisation?

The roster, assignments, attendance and workload live in the same place as the projects, so you can see who is over capacity and who has room for the next job. Tasks are classified as billable or otherwise and tagged by work type, and the ASPRF score gives a fair 30-day read on each person across six dimensions rather than raw hours.

### Is the firm's data kept private?

AORMS is cloud workspace at aorms.in with role-based access, so fees, client data and drawings stay inside the firm. The separate client, consultant and contractor portals are read-only and show outsiders only what you choose to share.
