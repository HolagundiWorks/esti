Architecture client portal software should reduce confusion, not create another
place to chase decisions. In an architecture practice, the client needs access to
drawings, approvals, invoices, RFIs and revision decisions, but they should not see
internal notes, staff workload, fee strategy or private firm records.

AORMS gives clients a scoped portal connected to the same project record your team
uses. The client sees only what is shared with them, and every approval or revision
request lands back on the office timeline.

## What a client portal should handle

- Drawing packages and issued revisions
- Approval requests with a clear status
- Client comments, decisions and acknowledgements
- Fee status, invoices and receipts where appropriate
- Project evidence without exposing internal operations

The portal works because it is not separate from the project. A drawing issue,
approval, client request and revision warning all belong to the same record.

## Why email and chat fail

Email and WhatsApp are useful for conversation, but weak for proof. A client can
say a drawing was never seen, an approval was not final, or a change was only a
small request. When decisions are scattered, the practice carries the risk.

In AORMS, the portal turns client movement into evidence. Requests are classified,
approvals are recorded, and major or critical revisions can warn the client that
cost, timeline or scope may be affected.

## Connected to the office

Client portal activity flows into project health, revision intelligence, fee
recovery and dashboard recommendations. That means the office does not only know
that a client logged in; it knows which decision is blocking work and who owns the
next action.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is architecture client portal software?

Architecture client portal software gives clients controlled access to project
drawings, approvals, revision requests, invoices and communication without exposing
the internal office system.

### Can clients approve drawings in AORMS?

Yes. Clients can review shared project material and respond through the portal, so
approvals and comments are recorded against the project instead of disappearing in
chat or email.

### Does the client see all office data?

No. AORMS uses scoped access. Clients see only the projects, documents and records
shared with them, while finance strategy, internal notes, staff work and firm
settings remain private.

### How does the portal help with revisions?

Revision requests are attached to the project record and can be classified by
impact. Major and critical changes can warn the client before the practice accepts
work that affects cost, scope or timeline.
