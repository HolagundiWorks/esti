# Estimate Like a Measurement Book, Not a Form

Most estimating tools make you stop and fill in a form for every line. Open a dialog, type a description, pick a unit, enter a quantity, save, repeat. An estimator who has spent twenty years with an Excel sheet and a measurement book finds that slower, not faster.

**Architecture estimation software** built for the way Indian practices actually price work should feel like the measurement book — keyboard-first, inline, and continuous. AORMS's estimation workspace does exactly that: think, type, calculate, continue.

## A keyboard-first BOQ, not a wizard

The costing window is a single workspace. Pick the **rate book** for the estimate in the side panel, then `+ Add Item` and start typing — the rate-book search filters as you type, you press Enter, and the BOQ line appears. There are no modals, no save buttons after every field, and no multi-step wizards. The rate comes straight from the rate book; the amount computes itself.

This is the difference between software that was designed around a database form and software that was designed around how an estimator actually works.

## The measurement book, built in

Every BOQ line opens a nested **measurement book** — the same `Element · Nos · Length · Breadth · Depth` table an estimator already keeps by hand. Type `Wall A`, then `2`, then `5`, then `3`, press Enter, and a new row opens for `Wall B`. Each row's quantity is calculated instantly, and the parent BOQ quantity is the running sum — written back to the line automatically.

The columns adapt to the line's unit: a square-metre item asks for length and breadth, a cubic-metre item adds depth, a running-metre item asks only for length. You never configure a formula; the unit decides what to measure.

Because the quantity is derived from the measurement book, the working-out is never lost. Reopen the estimate next week and every element — Wall A, Wall B, Wall C — is still there to edit, not a bare number you have to reverse-engineer.

## Rate books your office controls

Rates live in versioned **rate books** — your own schedule of rates, a state or CPWD reference, or a custom project book. One rate book is active per estimate, and changing it re-searches and re-rates. Rate analysis lets you build up a rate from material, labour and machinery components when a published rate does not exist. The numbers are India-native throughout: money is handled in paise, and totals format the way an Indian estimator expects.

## From estimate to site, on one record

The estimate is not a dead end. In the full office system it becomes the project BOQ, and the same record carries cost management through construction:

- **Tenders and bidding** — issue the BOQ to contractors, collect item-wise bids, compare and award.
- **Work packages** — the awarded scope becomes a priced work package.
- **Site measurement book** — measure work as it is done, approve it, and bill it on running-account bills with deductions netted to a payable.
- **Controls** — quantity and rate deviations and two-step variation orders keep the contract honest.
- **Final account and cost dashboard** — a live view of estimated vs tendered vs awarded vs billed vs certified, with overrun signals.

Because the estimate, the tender, the bill and the cost dashboard share one record, a cost overrun on site is visible against the figure you estimated at the start — not discovered at the end.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is architecture estimation software?

It is software that builds a bill of quantities (BOQ) and prices it for an architecture or construction project. AORMS's estimation workspace is keyboard-first: you pick rate-book items, build quantities in a measurement book element by element, and the line and project totals compute automatically — without forms, modals or a separate calculation screen.

### How does the measurement book work?

Each BOQ line opens a nested table of `Element · Nos · Length · Breadth · Depth`. You enter one row per element (Wall A, Wall B, …), each row's quantity is calculated as nos × the dimensions the unit needs, and the parent BOQ quantity is the sum — written back to the line. The dimension columns adapt to the line's unit, so you never configure a formula.

### Where do the rates come from?

From versioned rate books — your own schedule of rates, a CPWD or state reference, or a custom project rate book. One rate book is active per estimate. Rate analysis lets you build up a rate from material, labour and machinery components when no published rate exists.

### Does the estimate connect to tenders and billing?

Yes. The approved estimate becomes the project BOQ, which can be issued to contractors as a tender, awarded into a priced work package, measured and billed on running-account bills, and tracked on a cost dashboard that compares estimated, tendered, awarded, billed and certified values.
