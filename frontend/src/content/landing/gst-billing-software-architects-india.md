A wrong GST invoice is not a small problem for an architecture practice. Charge CGST/SGST when you should have charged IGST, miss the SAC code, forget the client's TDS deduction, or break the invoice number sequence, and the error surfaces months later � at GSTR-1 filing, or worse, in a notice. ## The place-of-supply split, done right

The single most common GST mistake on a professional invoice is the wrong tax split. AORMS applies it by place of supply:

- For an **intra-state** client � same state as your registered place of supply � the invoice splits the tax into CGST and SGST.
- For an **inter-state** client, it applies IGST instead.

You select the client and the supply; the system applies the correct heads. There is no manual toggling that someone forgets on a Friday-evening invoice.

## SAC code and TDS under 194J

Architectural and design work are services, so a GST invoice carries the relevant professional-services SAC code rather than an HSN code meant for goods. AORMS puts the SAC code on the invoice as standard.

Clients deducting tax at source on professional fees do so under section 194J. If your invoice ignores that, your receivable never matches the bank credit, and your books drift from Form 26AS all year. AORMS accounts for the TDS the client deducts, so the net receivable is what you actually expect to receive, and the deducted amount is tracked for reconciliation against 26AS later.

## Gap-free, FY-sequential numbering

GST rules expect invoice numbers to run in an unbroken sequence within the financial year. A gap or a duplicate is a red flag at filing. ### When do I charge CGST/SGST versus IGST?

You charge CGST and SGST when the place of supply is in your own state, and IGST when it is in another state. For architectural and design work the place of supply is where the **project site** is, under IGST Act section 12(3) — not the client's registered address. AORMS derives the split from the project's site state so you do not have to decide it manually on each invoice, and warns you if you override it.

### How does the software handle TDS on professional fees?

Clients deduct TDS on architectural fees under section 194J. AORMS accounts for that deduction on the invoice so your net receivable matches the bank credit, and tracks the deducted amount so you can reconcile it against Form 26AS at filing time.

### What SAC code applies to architectural services?

Architectural and design work are services and carry the relevant professional-services SAC code rather than a goods HSN code. AORMS includes the SAC code on invoices as standard. Always confirm the exact current code with your tax adviser, as classifications can change.
