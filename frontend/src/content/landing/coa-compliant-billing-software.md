# COA-Compliant Billing for Architects

Fees in an Indian practice are won at the proposal stage and lost in the billing. The proposal is written against the Council of Architecture Scale of Charges, broken into the familiar service stages, with a percentage of the total fee allotted to each. Then the project runs for two years, the proposal lives in a Word file nobody opens, and invoices get raised on gut feel. By the end, nobody can say cleanly how much of stage three has been billed. A **COA compliant architecture billing system** closes that gap by making the proposal the source of truth and tying every invoice back to the stage it belongs to.

AORMS, powered by ESTI, does exactly that — fee proposals follow the COA Scale of Charges, and billing is tied to phase milestones.

## Fees structured the COA way

The COA Scale of Charges divides an architect's services into stages — broadly concept and feasibility, preliminary design and estimates, drawings for approvals, working drawings and tender, and construction-stage services. Each stage carries a defined share of the total professional fee.

In AORMS a fee proposal is built on this structure rather than as a single lump sum. You set the total fee — whether as a percentage of project cost, per square foot, or a negotiated figure — and distribute it across the COA service stages. The proposal then reads the way the COA intends: a clear schedule of what each stage costs and when it falls due.

Because the proposal carries the stage breakdown, two things become possible that a flat quote can never offer:

- You always know how much fee remains unbilled at each stage.
- A client or partner can see the basis of every rupee, which makes fee conversations far shorter.

## Billing against phase milestones

The point of structuring fees by stage is to bill them by stage. AORMS ties billing to phase milestones, so an invoice is raised when a phase is actually delivered, not on an arbitrary date.

Projects in AORMS run on phases. As a phase reaches its milestone, the fee allotted to that COA stage becomes billable. You raise the invoice against the proposal, the system knows how much of that stage's fee is being claimed, and the running balance updates. There is no double-billing a stage and no forgetting to bill one, because the proposal and the project share the same record.

This is the discipline most offices try to keep in a spreadsheet and lose within a quarter. Here it is structural.

## GST and TDS, correct on every invoice

A COA-structured fee still has to become a tax-correct invoice. AORMS handles that without a separate step:

- The CGST/SGST split for an intra-state client, or IGST for an inter-state one, is applied by place of supply.
- The professional-services SAC code is on the invoice.
- TDS deducted by the client under section 194J is accounted for, so the receivable matches what actually lands in the bank.
- Invoice numbers are gap-free and FY-sequential on the April–March financial year, which is what filing demands.

Money is held in integer paise, so a milestone that is, say, 18.75 percent of a fee does not round into a discrepancy over the life of the project. The tax mechanics are covered in depth on the [GST billing software for architects](/gst-billing-software-architects-india) page; here the focus is the fee structure that feeds it.

## From proposal to filing, one thread

Because the proposal, phases, invoices and tax all sit in one system, the whole chain holds together:

- Win the work with a COA-stage fee proposal.
- Deliver a phase, bill the stage against its milestone.
- Raise a GST- and TDS-correct invoice with the right SAC code and a clean sequential number.
- Reconcile receipts against bank, 26AS, AIS and GSTR imports, and produce GSTR-1, GSTR-3B and TDS filing abstracts at period end.
- Track purchase orders for outflows alongside.

Add the immutable audit log and activity timeline, and every fee decision and invoice is accountable after the fact — useful when a client queries a claim months later.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is a COA compliant architecture billing system?

It is billing software that structures fees the way the Council of Architecture Scale of Charges does — by service stage, with each stage carrying a defined share of the total fee — and then bills each stage as it is delivered. AORMS builds fee proposals on COA service stages and ties invoicing to phase milestones, so the proposal and the invoices stay aligned throughout the project.

### How are COA service stages used for billing?

In AORMS the total fee is distributed across COA stages in the fee proposal. As each project phase reaches its milestone, the fee allotted to that stage becomes billable, and you raise an invoice against it. The system tracks how much of each stage has been billed, so nothing is double-claimed or left out.

### Does milestone billing work with GST and TDS?

Yes. Every milestone invoice applies the correct CGST/SGST or IGST split, carries the professional-services SAC code, accounts for TDS under section 194J, and uses gap-free FY-sequential numbering. The fee structure and the tax treatment live in the same invoice rather than being reconciled separately.

### Can I set fees as a percentage of project cost or per square foot?

The fee proposal supports the basis your practice uses, whether a percentage of project cost, a per-square-foot rate or a negotiated figure, then distributes that total across the COA service stages. Money is handled in integer paise so percentage-based milestone amounts do not drift over the project's life.

### How is this different from a generic billing tool?

A generic tool bills lump sums or flat line items and has no concept of COA stages or Indian tax. A COA compliant architecture billing system understands service stages, ties them to phase delivery, and produces GST- and TDS-correct invoices ready for GSTR and TDS filing — all on the April–March financial year.
