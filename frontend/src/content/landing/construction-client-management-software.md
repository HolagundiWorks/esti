## Construction Client Management Software

Most software for architects manages the design phase and then goes quiet. The relationship that strains a practice is often during **Construction Administration** — when the client wants to know why a drawing changed, a contractor query needs a client decision, a site instruction has fee implications, and progress must stay visible. **Construction client management** here means keeping the client informed and accountable through CA, with a record behind every decision — not running contractor RA bills or PMC portfolios.

AORMS, powered by ESTI, carries the client relationship from proposal through architect-side site supervision in one system.

## A scoped client portal through the build

During construction the client should see progress without wandering through your office. AORMS gives them a **client portal** scoped to their project: issued drawings, transmittals, approvals waiting on them, invoices, and project status. They stay informed, you stop fielding "can you send me the latest drawing?" emails, and everything they saw is recorded. The portal itself is covered on the [client management](/architecture-client-management-software) page.

## Approvals and RFIs that don't stall the site

Work stalls when a decision sits in someone's inbox. AORMS keeps approvals on record — what was sent, the drawing revision, the date and the client's response — so a delayed sign-off is visible. Contractors get a scoped portal for documents and site coordination (RFIs, submittals, snags, NCRs), so queries route to the firm with a trail. You coordinate client and contractor from one advisory record — not a tender or contractor ERP.

## Site instructions and progress, with the client in the loop

Construction Administration is where money and scope move fastest for the **consultant**. AORMS logs site instructions, snags, and progress reports against the project so fee implications and client awareness stay captured — rather than agreed verbally and forgotten. The delivery side is detailed on the [office management](/architecture-office-management-software) page. AORMS does **not** replace contractor running-bill / RA accounting.

## Changes during construction stay billable

CA is when "small changes" multiply. Because AORMS logs every change with its [revision source and category](/client-revision-management) and ties it to the [COA fee proposal](/coa-compliant-billing-software), a client-driven change during the build is visible against the agreed scope — so it can be billed, not absorbed. The [revision tracking](/architecture-revision-tracking) page covers the mechanics.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is construction client management software?

Software that keeps the client informed and accountable during Construction Administration — sharing drawings, transmittals, approvals, invoices and progress through a scoped portal, and recording every decision — rather than only managing the design stage. AORMS does this for architecture consultancies; it is not contractor billing software.

### How do clients see progress during construction?

Through a client portal scoped to their project: issued drawings and transmittals, pending approvals, invoices and status. They stay informed without access to the rest of the office, and everything shared is recorded.

### How are contractor RFIs and the client connected?

Contractors get a scoped portal for site coordination — RFIs, submittals, snags and NCRs route to the firm with a trail, and decisions that need client input are recorded as approvals.

### Does AORMS handle contractor running bills?

No. Contractor RA / running-bill accounting is out of scope. AORMS tracks architect-side site instructions, progress reports, and GST fee invoices to the client.

### Do construction-phase changes stay billable?

They do. Changes are logged with their revision source and category and tied to the COA fee proposal, so a client-driven change during CA is visible against the agreed scope and can be raised as additional fee.
