# Client Revision Management for Architects

Ask any architect where the **revision frustration** comes from and the answer is the same: the client who keeps changing their mind, the "small tweak" that is the fourth small tweak this week, and the moment months later when they insist they never asked for any of it. Unmanaged client revisions do not just waste design hours — they erode the fee, the timeline and the relationship, and there is rarely a record to point to. **Client revision management** is about turning that mess into a record you can stand behind.

AORMS, powered by ESTI, treats every revision as data, not noise.

## Every change carries its source and category

When a decision changes in AORMS, it is logged with two things that matter:

- **Revision source** — was this *client-driven*, an *internal error*, a *technical query*, or a *scope change*? This is the field that ends the "we never asked for that" argument.
- **Revision category** — *minor*, *major*, or *critical*, so the weight of a change is recorded, not just its existence.

Those two fields feed a **Revision Intelligence** view that shows, at a glance, how much of your rework is genuinely client-driven versus your own errors or technical queries. For the first time you can say — with evidence — "62% of this project's revisions came from the client after sign-off." The mechanics of the log are detailed on the [architecture revision tracking](/architecture-revision-tracking) page.

## The approval trail that protects you

A revision you cannot prove is a revision you will eat. AORMS keeps an **immutable approval and activity log**: what was issued, when, on which drawing, and who signed off. Drawing revisions are tracked against the register, and approvals sent to the client are recorded with their response. When the dispute starts, you open the record instead of searching your inbox. The approval side is on the [approval workflow](/architecture-approval-workflow-software) page.

## Making scope creep billable

The real cost of poor client revision management is the unbilled change. If a client-driven revision after a phase sign-off is a scope change, it is a billable event — but only if it was captured as one. Because AORMS ties revisions to decisions, projects and the [COA fee proposal](/coa-compliant-billing-software), a major client-driven change is visible against the agreed scope, so you can raise it as additional fee instead of absorbing it silently. The [change management](/architecture-change-management-software) page covers the workflow.

## From frustration to a calm record

The point is not to fight clients — it is to remove the ambiguity that causes the friction. When every change has a source, a category, an approval and a link to the fee, the conversation shifts from "did you ask for this?" to "here is the record, and here is the additional fee for the scope change you approved." That is the difference between a frustrating relationship and a professional one.

## Frequently asked questions

### How does AORMS reduce revision frustration?

It removes the ambiguity that causes the friction. Every change is logged with its source (client-driven, internal error, technical query, scope change) and category (minor, major, critical), with an approval trail behind it — so disputes are settled by the record, and client-driven scope changes become visible and billable rather than silently absorbed.

### What is client revision management?

It is the practice of capturing every client-requested change with enough context — who asked, when, how significant, and whether it was approved — that the firm can defend its work and bill for scope creep. AORMS does this with revision source/category fields, drawing revision tracking and an immutable approval log.

### Can I prove a client approved a change?

Yes. Approvals are recorded with what was issued, the drawing revision, the date and the client's response, in an immutable log — so you can show exactly what was approved and when.

### How do I bill clients for extra revisions?

When a client-driven revision after sign-off is a scope change, AORMS shows it against the agreed COA fee proposal so you can raise it as additional fee. The [change management](/architecture-change-management-software) workflow ties the change to the billing.

### Does it show how much rework is the client's fault?

The Revision Intelligence view breaks revisions down by source, so you can see what share of rework was client-driven versus internal errors or technical queries — useful for both fee conversations and improving your own process.
