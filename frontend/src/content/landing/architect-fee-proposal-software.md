Architect fee proposal software should help a practice do more than prepare a
quotation. A fee proposal is a commercial promise: what the architect will do, how
the work is staged, when billing happens and what the client has approved.

AORMS connects fee proposals to the wider office record. A proposal can sit beside
the project, phase plan, approvals, invoices, receipts and dashboard signals that
show whether the practice is recovering value from completed work.

## What a proposal must control

A useful architecture fee proposal should record:

- Project and client
- Scope and exclusions
- Service stages or milestones
- Fee basis and breakup
- Reimbursables and special terms
- Approval status
- Billing readiness

When these live in a spreadsheet, they are easy to calculate but hard to manage.
The office still needs someone to remember when a stage is complete and whether an
invoice has been raised.

approved stages can become billing prompts instead of hidden spreadsheet rows.

## Why architects need a specific tool

Architecture services are rarely one-line invoices. A project may include concept,
schematic design, approvals, working drawings, site coordination and
revision-heavy client work. Generic billing tools do not understand this
relationship between scope, stage and evidence.

AORMS connects the proposal to the work that justifies it. That gives the owner a
better answer to a hard question: what have we delivered, what can we bill, and

## Where fee leakage begins

Fee leakage rarely begins with one large mistake. It begins with small moments the
office does not record. A client asks for another option. A stage is completed but
not billed. A drawing package is issued, but the fee milestone remains buried in a
spreadsheet. A partner remembers the follow-up two weeks late. By then, the client
has moved on to the next demand.

AORMS keeps fee proposals close to project movement. When delivery progresses, the
commercial record remains visible. The principal can see which stages are ready
for billing, which invoices are pending, and which client or project has begun to
consume more work than the approved fee justifies.

## A proposal as practice memory

A good proposal is not only a number. It is a memory of scope. It records what the
architect agreed to do, what is excluded, what assumptions were made and what the
client accepted. When this memory is separate from the project record, the office
has to defend scope from scattered documents.

Inside AORMS, the fee proposal can sit beside decisions, drawings, revisions and
invoices. That makes commercial review much clearer. If a project has many
client-driven changes, the proposal is not far away. If a stage is ready to bill,
the invoice workflow is not far away. If the client questions a fee, the office
can trace the work that led to it.

## Better conversations with clients

The goal is not to make every interaction feel rigid. The goal is to help the
architect speak clearly. "This was included", "this is additional", "this stage is
complete", and "this amount is now billable" become easier statements when the
record supports them.

For Indian practices, this clarity matters even more because fee proposals,
professional invoices, GST, TDS and receipts often live in different tools. AORMS
brings those parts closer so the office can manage design value and financial
discipline together.

## Fee recovery signals, always visible

When proposals, delivery and billing share one record, the office gains the
commercial signals that matter most:

- **What needs invoicing now** — stages delivered but not yet billed, surface as
  actionable items rather than buried spreadsheet rows.
- **What is due next** — the upcoming billing stage for each project, so the team
  can plan outreach before the milestone rather than after.
- **Incoming receipts** — payments expected from approved invoices, visible against
  outstanding amounts so cash flow is readable.
- **GST reminders** — upcoming GSTR filing dates and TDS deductions tracked
  alongside the invoices they belong to, so nothing falls silent between quarters.

## What owners can review

Owners do not need another decorative proposal template. They need to know which
fees are approved, which stages are complete, which work is waiting to be billed,
which invoices are pending and which clients need follow-up. AORMS makes those
questions part of the same office record that holds projects, approvals and
revisions.

This gives the practice a better commercial rhythm. Fee recovery becomes a weekly
discipline rather than a month-end rescue exercise. The office can still design
with generosity, but it no longer has to forget the value of its own work.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is architect fee proposal software?

It is software that helps architecture practices prepare, approve and track
professional fee proposals by project, scope, stage and billing milestone.

### Does AORMS connect proposals to invoices?

Yes. AORMS keeps fee proposals connected to billing workflows so completed work

about GST filing dates — the signals that make fee recovery a weekly habit.
