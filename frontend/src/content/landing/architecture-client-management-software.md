# Keep Every Client On The Record

The hardest part of running an architecture practice is rarely the design. It is the client relationship: the conversation that never made it into writing, the approval given on a call and forgotten, the fee that was agreed verbally and then disputed at invoicing. Most studios manage all of this in inboxes and memory, and that is exactly where it leaks.

**Architect client management software** built for Indian practices puts the whole relationship on one record — who the client is, what was proposed, what they agreed, and what they have decided — so the relationship is documented rather than remembered.

## A CRM that knows architecture

ESTI's client module is a CRM shaped for an architecture practice, not a sales pipeline borrowed from another industry. Each client has a record, and every interaction is captured in the client log — calls, meetings, site visits, decisions. When a client phones to ask about a change, the person picking up can see the full history in one place instead of hunting through three people's inboxes.

The client log matters most at the moments that usually go undocumented: a verbal go-ahead, a request to "just move that wall", a change of mind on a finish. Recorded against the client, these become part of the project's history rather than a he-said dispute six months later.

## Proposals and fees the client can trust

The relationship usually begins with a proposal and a fee. ESTI handles both as proper office documents:

- **Proposals** — project proposals captured as records, not loose PDFs.
- **Fee proposals** — built on the COA Scale of Charges, with billing tied to phase milestones so the client sees exactly what they pay for and when.

When work is delivered, invoices are produced GST-correct — CGST/SGST or IGST as the place of supply requires, TDS under section 194J, the right SAC code, and gap-free FY-sequential numbering. A client receiving an ESTI invoice gets a document that holds up to their accountant's scrutiny, which is its own form of relationship management. ## The read-only client portal

Email is where client communication goes to die. A drawing sent on Tuesday is buried by Friday; an approval requested gets a one-word reply that nobody files. ESTI gives each client a separate, read-only portal instead.

Through the portal a client can see the project as you choose to present it and — crucially — **submit decisions and approvals** that land directly on the record. When a client approves a drawing or signs off a decision through the portal, that approval is timestamped and attached to the project. There is no separate step to "file" it; the act of approving is the record.

This changes the dynamic of client sign-off. Instead of chasing a reply and then transcribing it, the approval arrives already documented, linked to the drawing or decision it relates to. And because the portal is read-only, the client sees what they need without touching the working data.

## Decisions and revisions, attributed fairly

Client-driven change is a normal part of architecture, but it is only manageable if it is recorded. ESTI ties client decisions into the project's revision history: when a revision is caused by the client, it is sourced as CLIENT_DRIVEN, distinct from an internal error or a technical query. Over a project, that distinction is the difference between absorbing rework silently and having an honest conversation — backed by the record — about scope and additional fees.

Threaded comments and critical notes sit alongside, so internal discussion about a client matter stays connected to the client and the project rather than scattered across chat.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is architect client management software?

It is software that keeps the architect–client relationship on one record — the client log, proposals and fee proposals, invoices, and the decisions and approvals a client makes. ESTI's architect client management software is built for Indian practices, so fees follow the COA Scale of Charges and invoices are GST-correct.

### How do clients approve drawings or decisions?

Each client gets a separate, read-only portal. Through it they can view the project and submit decisions and approvals, which land directly on the project record with a timestamp — so a sign-off is documented at the moment it happens, with no separate filing step.

### Is the client portal able to edit our working data?

No. The client portal is read-only by design. Clients see what you choose to present and can submit approvals and decisions, but they cannot alter the underlying drawings, fees or project data, which stay under your role-based access control.

### How does it help with client-driven changes and extra fees?

When a revision is caused by the client, it is recorded with the source CLIENT_DRIVEN, separate from internal errors or technical queries. Over a project this gives you an honest, on-record picture of how much rework was client-driven — the basis for a fair conversation about additional fees.

### Are the invoices compliant for Indian clients?

Yes. Invoices are produced with correct CGST/SGST or IGST, TDS under section 194J, the right SAC code and gap-free FY-sequential numbering on the April–March financial year — documents that stand up to a client's accountant.

### Where is our client data stored?

ESTI is self-hosted with role-based access, so your client records, communication log and fee data remain on infrastructure your practice controls rather than a third-party cloud.
