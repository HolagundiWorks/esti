## Run Your Architecture Office in India

An architecture office in India runs on a dozen things at once: fee milestones to bill, invoices to follow up, client revisions to track, authority approvals to chase, attendance to mark and a client asking where their drawings are. Most offices manage this across a project tool that ignores Indian practice, an accountant's separate ledger and a group chat. **Architecture office management India** practices can stand on means putting the commercial, revision-tracking and operational sides in one record — built for Indian offices, not adapted to them.

AORMS, powered by ESTI, is that system: a cloud-hosted Architecture Office Resource Management System where fee recovery, revision control, projects, teams and client portals share one source of truth.

## Fee recovery, visible across the office

Indian projects stall when fee milestones are buried in spreadsheets and nobody can answer "what is ready to invoice today?" In AORMS, fee proposals are built on the COA Scale of Charges and billing is tied to phase delivery — so the office can always see what is invoiceable now, what stage comes next, what is incoming and which clients have outstanding dues.

GST-correct invoices — CGST/SGST or IGST by place of supply, TDS under section 194J, the professional-services SAC code and gap-free FY-sequential numbering — are produced from the same record as the proposal that justifies them. GSTR-1, GSTR-3B and TDS filing abstracts are built in, so filing season is a review step rather than a reconstruction.

## Client revision tracking — from meeting to site

A client instruction that bypasses the record is where practices lose fee and lose arguments. AORMS keeps every revision in a deliberate sequence:

1. Meeting minutes are recorded on the project and issued to the client portal.
2. ESTI extracts each change as a draft revision request.
3. The client reviews and submits the real ones.
4. The architect marks criticality and implications.
5. The client approves the architect's assessment through the portal.
6. Only then do site or drawing changes proceed.

That sequence means every change is categorised, approved and traceable — not absorbed silently. Client-driven revisions that become scope changes are visible and billable. For the full story, see [minutes of meeting software for architects](/minutes-of-meeting-software-for-architects) and [architecture revision tracking](/architecture-revision-tracking).

## Authority and site records

Indian projects stall when authority notes, client approvals and site records are held outside the office system. AORMS keeps those records inside the project instead of in a consultant's head.

Permit assumptions, authority comments, project evidence and client decisions stay beside drawings, site notes and fee records. That gives the principal a cleaner view of what has been promised, what is pending and what must be reviewed before the project moves.
