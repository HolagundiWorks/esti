Most “early warning” talk in architecture offices is informal — a WhatsApp ping,
a corridor note, a principal’s memory. By the time a risk is written down, the
drawing set is already mid-issue and the fee stage is already half spent.

**AORMS-Studio** treats pre-construction **risks and opportunities (R&O)** as a
first-class register on the project, not a slide in a kickoff deck.

## What the register is for

The pre-con R&O spine sits on the project with the brief and design gates. It is
not a construction programme or a contractor Gantt. It answers advisory questions:

- What could hurt fee, liability, or issue readiness if we ignore it?
- What opportunity improves margin, clarity, or client value if we chase it?
- Has the team assessed probability and impact — or only named a worry?
- Are we ready to issue, with reliance and fee stage paths confirmed?

## Gates, not vibes

Entries move through explicit readiness thinking — from early brief capture
through concept assessment toward **issue readiness**: issue class, reliance, and
the fee stage billable path are confirmed before the set leaves the office.

That is the same philosophy as revision control and drawing approvals: **name the
gate**, date the decision, keep the evidence on the project record.

## How it fits the spine

R&O lives beside project Info, phases, drawings, and fee stages. Studio
Intelligence can surface blockers that sit in the register the same way it
surfaces overdue approvals or unbilled stages. Engineering consultancies on
**AORMS-Consultancy** share the same platform pattern when that workspace launches.
