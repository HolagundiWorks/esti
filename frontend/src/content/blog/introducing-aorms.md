Most architecture practices do not fail on design. They fail on the quiet
operational drift between projects — an approval that sat too long, an invoice
that never went out, a drawing set waiting on a consultant who went silent.

AORMS exists to surface that drift **before it matures into a problem**.

## One operating picture

Instead of a dozen disconnected tools, AORMS reads your office as a single
system: clients, projects, billing, approvals, revisions, drawings, and team
load. It reasons across them, predicts where pressure is building, and
recommends the single highest-leverage action for the day.

## Calm by design

The dashboard is intentionally quiet. Most of the office is under control most
of the time, so the screen says so. When something genuinely needs you, it says
that too — once, clearly, with the next step attached.

> The goal is not more data. The goal is **useful guidance despite imperfect
> office data**.

That is AORMS.
