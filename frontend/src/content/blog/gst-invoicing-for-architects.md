Architectural services in India are billed under **SAC 998322**. Getting the
tax split right is less about the rate and more about a handful of rules that are
easy to miss when you are invoicing between projects — and getting them wrong is
the most reliable way to delay fee recovery or fail a filing reconciliation.

## Intra-state vs inter-state

- If your firm and the client are in the **same state**, the GST splits into
  **CGST + SGST**.
- If they are in **different states**, it is a single **IGST** line.

Mixing these up is the most common reason a GST abstract refuses to reconcile at
filing time.

## TDS and net receivable

Many corporate clients deduct **TDS** on professional fees. Your invoice total
and your *net receivable* are therefore different numbers — and your cash-flow
view should track the receivable, not the headline total.

## Composition scheme

Firms under the composition scheme issue a **bill of supply**, not a tax
invoice, and pay a fixed **composition levy** rather than collecting GST line by
line. The document kind has to match the scheme, or the client's books will flag
it.
