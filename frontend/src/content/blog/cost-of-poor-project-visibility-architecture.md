"Visibility" became a software buzzword. For architecture studios, poor visibility is
simpler and more expensive: **the principal cannot answer four questions without
calling three people**:

1. What fee is at risk on Project X?
2. Which drawings are blocked waiting for client approval?
3. Who is overloaded this fortnight?
4. What site issues are open on commissions finishing this quarter?

If those answers take a day to assemble, the studio already pays a **visibility tax**
every week.

## The tax shows up in three currencies

### Margin

Unbilled stages, unlogged variations, and delayed invoices are visibility failures —
not sales failures. The work happened; the office did not **see** it was billable in time.

### Trust

Clients lose confidence when the firm cannot state approval status or issue history
cleanly. Consultants duplicate work on outdated sheets. Trust repairs cost more than
preventing the confusion.

### Principal burnout

When the partner is the integration layer — walking corridors to build a mental picture
— strategic work shrinks. Burnout is a visibility failure at org design level.

## Why visibility is hard without a practice spine

Data is **event-rich** but **integration-poor**:

- Tasks know hours; they do not know invoice milestones.
- Drawings know revisions; they do not know fee impact of pending changes.
- Site photos know snags; they do not know which sheet they invalidate.

Dashboards that only chart tasks repeat the fragmentation with prettier charts.

## What good visibility looks like

| View | Decision it enables |
|------|---------------------|
| Billing-ready phases | Invoice this week vs defer |
| Overdue client approvals | Chase before issue date slips |
| Revision fee exposure | Scope conversation now |
| Workload by person | Reassign before quality drops |
| Open site items | Site visit agenda |

Studio Intelligence in AORMS groups these as **zones** with action-ranked tables —
designed for a five-minute principal review, not a quarterly report.

## Improving visibility without a big-bang

1. **One source per signal** — pick the system of record for fees, drawings, revisions.
2. **Close the loop weekly** — billing-ready must map to invoices or explicit deferral.
3. **Portal discipline** — client approvals visible in the same record as drawings.
4. **Train deputies** — document the morning review on the
   [wiki](https://aorms.in/wiki/how-to-use-aorms).

## Frequently asked questions

### Do we need BI tools?

Only after the operational record is trustworthy. BI on fragmented Excel multiplies noise.

### What about partner intuition?

Intuition is valuable; it should not be the **only** integration layer.
