The site call that blows up the programme. The invoice that should have gone out last
month. The drawing set issued with a revision someone thought was "just internal."
Principals often describe these as surprises. In hindsight, the signals were usually
there — scattered across WhatsApp, email, and one architect's notebook.

This article explains **why** teams miss early warnings, and what an office record
needs to surface them while there is still time to act.

## Signals arrive fragmented

Warning signs in architecture are rarely one alarm. They are **weak correlations**:

| Signal | Often lives in |
|--------|----------------|
| Fee not billed for completed stage | Partner's memory / spreadsheet |
| Client approval overdue | Email thread |
| Task overload on one drafter | Individual's calendar |
| Revision budget exhausted | Redlines on PDF |
| Site snag unresolved | Site engineer's phone gallery |

No single person sees all five. The principal sees fee anxiety; the project
architect sees redlines; HR sees attendance — but nobody sees the **composite risk**
until something breaks.

## The four early warnings principals should not miss

### 1. Billing-ready work with no invoice

Design stages complete in tasks and decisions, but invoices lag. Cash flow suffers
quietly. **Action:** a dashboard that lists billing-ready phases by project.

### 2. Open high-impact revisions near issue dates

Major client changes still "in discussion" while the team prepares issue drawings.
**Action:** revision register with category and approval state tied to drawing rows.

### 3. Workload concentration

One senior carries three projects in CD simultaneously. Quality and deadlines slip
together. **Action:** workload view across tasks with estimated hours.

### 4. Site feedback not linked to drawings

Snags and site instructions live outside the drawing register. Field mistakes
repeat. **Action:** site modules on the same project record as drawings and
transmittals.

## Why generic PM tools fail here

Tools built for generic task lists do not understand **COA stages**, **transmittals**,
**GST invoices**, or **client portals**. Teams bolt on spreadsheets for fees and
WhatsApp for approvals — recreating fragmentation inside another app.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do small studios need a dashboard?

Yes — especially small studios, where one person plays principal, PM, and biller.
Automation replaces the "principal's head" as the integration layer.

### What if we already use a scheduling tool or generic task tracker?

Keep them for schedules if they work. The gap is usually **commercial and approval**
data, not Gantt bars. Integrate or replace the record layer, not every tool.
