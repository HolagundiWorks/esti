Most practice-management tools are built for somewhere else and translated for
India by adding a rupee symbol. The hard parts — the ones that decide whether
your books reconcile and your drawings get sanctioned — are left to you and your
CA. The **India desk** is the part of AORMS that handles them natively.

## Money that adds up

All money is stored and handled in integer **paise**, so rounding never quietly
drifts the way floating-point rupees do. Invoices know the difference between
**intra-state** (CGST + SGST) and **inter-state** (IGST), apply **SAC 998322** for
architectural services, account for **TDS** so your net receivable is right, and
issue a **bill of supply** with a composition levy when the firm is under the
composition scheme.

## A year that runs April to March

Financial year, filing windows, GST and TDS due dates — all keyed to the Indian
calendar, surfaced on the dashboard so a return never sneaks up on you.

## Local from the ground up

- **BBMP and jurisdiction bylaws** for FAR, coverage and setbacks.
- **DSR** (schedule of rates) reference for estimation and BOQ.
- **COA fee logic** for fee proposals.

The India desk is not a localisation layer bolted on at the end. It is the
assumption the whole system is built on — because a practice in Bengaluru should
not have to fight its software to stay compliant.
